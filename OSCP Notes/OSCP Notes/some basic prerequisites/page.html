• SUBNETTING
    • Number of bits that are turned on in a Subnet Mask is its called CIDR value.
    • Number of Hosts depends upon the number of bits turned off in a Subnet Mask.
    • The rule for turning on the bits in a subnet mask is consecutive from right to left.
    • CIDR stands for Class-less inter Domain Routing.
    • If a CIDR value is something let's say 24 then it can also be called as Whack 24 or /24 Network.
    • Number of hosts from a subnet mask also helps in determining the final broadcast id.
  • The IANA (Internet Assigned Numbers Authority)  has primarily been responsible for assigning address blocks to the five regional internet registries or RIRs.
  • Autonomous System (AS) Numbers are used by various routing protocols. IANA allocates ASN to Regional Internet Registries (RIRs)  
  • The five RIRs are:
    • AFRINIC, which serves the continent of Africa
    • ARIN, which serves the United States, Canada and parts of the Caribbean
    • APNIC, which is responsible for most of Asia, Australia and New Zealand and Pacific Island nations
    • LACNIC, which covers Central and South America and any parts of the Caribbean not covered by ARIN
    • RIPE, which serves Europe, Russia,the Middle East, and portions of Central Asia
  • - The RIRs further allocate or assign AS Numbers to network operators in line with RIR policies.
  • - These five RIRs have been responsible for assigning IP address blocks to organizations within their geographic areas.
  • Small organizations don't have dedicated CIDR Range.
